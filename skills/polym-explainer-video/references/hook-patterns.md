# Hook patterns for short-form vertical video

The first 3 seconds determine whether a viewer keeps watching or scrolls. The hook is not optional — it's the most important segment in the entire video.

## The 4 hook patterns that work

### 1. Pain question
Open with the audience's biggest frustration, framed as a statement they'll nod at.

**Examples:**
- "Building AI agents from scratch is brutal." (DeerFlow — pain of agent infra)
- "Your CRM is a graveyard of dead leads." (sales tool)
- "Manual data entry kills 6 hours a week." (productivity SaaS)

**When to use:** product solves a clear pain. Audience research must show this pain is widely felt — confirm it in Phase 2 before using.

**Visual pairing:** A-roll of avatar saying it directly to camera, slight frustrated expression. NOT abstract — direct address.

### 2. Bold claim
Open with a specific, almost-unbelievable promise. Triggers "wait, what?" reaction.

**Examples:**
- "We just made a 60-minute analyst report in 4 minutes."
- "This is the only AI agent harness you'll ever need."
- "0 to 61.5k stars in 3 months. This is why."

**When to use:** product has a strong measurable outcome OR you have data to back the claim.

**Risk:** if claim is too vague ("we make AI better"), it's worse than no hook. Specificity is the whole game.

**Visual pairing:** number/data flash on screen + voice from avatar OR voiceover. Often best as HyperFrames hook with audio overlay.

### 3. Visual surprise
Skip the talking head entirely. Open with a striking visual that demands attention.

**Examples:**
- Split-screen: chaotic before / clean after, with a sharp slam-cut
- A counter rapidly climbing from 0 to a big number with sound effect
- A timeline collapsing, showing "what used to take a week, now: 4 minutes"
- A swarm of icons converging into one (consolidation message)

**When to use:** product story is fundamentally about a transformation, comparison, or scale change.

**Visual pairing:** Pure HyperFrames in first 3s — NO avatar. Voice can come in at 3s mark.

### 4. Curiosity gap
Open with a question that the audience needs the rest of the video to answer.

**Examples:**
- "Why is this open-source repo trending #1 on GitHub right now?"
- "What's the one thing every AI agent gets wrong?"
- "If you've ever built a chatbot, you know this problem..."

**When to use:** product is in a category the audience knows but maybe doesn't follow this specific entry. Curiosity gap pulls them in to learn what's new.

**Visual pairing:** A-roll of avatar asking the question, eyes engaged. The viewer should feel they're being addressed personally.

---

## Hook anti-patterns (NEVER use)

These are common but they tank retention:

- **"Hi everyone, today I want to talk about..."** — generic intro, immediate scroll
- **"Have you ever wondered..."** — too soft, sounds like a YouTube ad
- **"In this video..."** — meta-commentary about the video itself
- **Slow logo reveal** — only effective for established brands; unknowns waste 3 seconds
- **Long product name pronunciation** — "Welcome to DeerFlow, the one-stop..." — by the time the name is spoken, viewer is gone
- **Soft / non-specific claims** — "AI is changing everything" tells the viewer nothing
- **Question with obvious yes answer** — "Want to save time?" — patronizing

## Hook calibration to audience

The right hook depends on who the audience is. From the Phase 2 research, identify:

| Audience type | Best hook pattern | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| Engineers / builders | Pain question (specific technical pain) | Dry, technical, no fluff |
| Founders / executives | Bold claim with metric | Confident, outcome-focused |
| Mainstream consumers | Visual surprise | Energetic, sensory |
| Niche communities | Curiosity gap (insider reference) | Conversational, knowing |

If the Phase 2 research shows the audience values authenticity above polish (common for OSS / dev tools), favor pain question. If they value status / outcomes (common for B2B SaaS), favor bold claim. If they're scrolling casual feeds (consumer apps), favor visual surprise.

---

## Hook timing rules

- **Hook must complete by 3.0 seconds.** Not 3.5, not 4. The platform's autoplay decision happens at ~3s.
- **First word should be on-screen by 0.5s.** If your A-roll has a 1-second silent warm-up before the avatar speaks, cut it in post.
- **Do NOT show the product name in the first 3 seconds.** Save the brand reveal for second 4-6 — the hook earns the right to mention the product.
- **No music intro before voice.** Music can fade up under the voice but should never play alone for >0.3s before words start.

---

## Hook validation checklist

Before approving a hook in Phase 3, ask:

1. Could a viewer who has never heard of this product understand the FIRST sentence on its own?
2. Is the specificity high (numbers, concrete pain, specific scenario) rather than abstract?
3. Does the hook directly tie to a finding from Phase 2 social listening, or is it generic?
4. If you removed the hook, would the rest of the video still make sense? (If yes, the hook isn't doing its job.)

If any answer is "no", revise.
