## Evidence Classification Rules

1. OBSERVED = directly stated in the signal (only what is explicit and verifiable)
2. INFERRED = logical deductions from the signal (mark clearly as inference, not fact)
3. UNKNOWNS = data needed to make a confident decision but absent from the signal

**OBSERVED** — directly stated in signal or Trigify note:
- Explicit job title and company
- The interaction type (liked, commented, posted)
- Quoted content from the signal
- Stated company industry, size, or location
- Explicit "new to role" mention

**INFERRED** — logical deduction:
- "Company likely produces high-volume video because..."
- "Role suggests this person owns the production budget"
- "Short drama company = high-volume short video output by definition"

**UNKNOWNS** — needed but absent:
- Time in current role (unless stated)
- Company headcount (unless stated)
- Current tooling / existing video workflow
- Budget authority confirmation
- Specific pain point (when only inferred from engagement)

Key rule: Do NOT put tentative language in hard_disqualifiers.
Only confirmed facts belong there. Uncertainty always goes in unknowns.

## Examples

**Example — OBSERVED:**
"Person is Head of Production at a film studio" → OBSERVED (stated in profile)

**Example — INFERRED:**
"Role suggests ownership of production workflow decisions" → INFERRED (deduced from title)

**Example — UNKNOWN:**
"Time in current role is unknown" → UNKNOWN (absent but relevant)
"Current production tooling is unknown" → UNKNOWN (needed to confirm pain fit)
