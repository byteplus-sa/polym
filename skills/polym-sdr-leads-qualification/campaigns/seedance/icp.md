## Seedance ICP

**Seedance** is ByteDance's AI video generation platform.
Core capability: generate and edit high-quality video using AI — faster, cheaper, and at greater scale than a traditional production team.

## Primary Target Verticals

These are the exact priority verticals. Leads in these segments score highest.

| Segment ID | Description | Why Seedance fits |
|------------|-------------|------------------|
| **film_studio** | Film production studios, indie studios, feature/short film companies | Replace expensive post-production steps; generate B-roll, visual effects, transitions with AI |
| **film_production** | Production companies, commercial production houses, video production agencies | Scale output without adding editors; AI-generate content variants faster than manual |
| **broadcast_network** | Broadcast networks, TV networks, and major broadcasters with in-house production and promo teams | High-volume production of promos, trailers, explainers, news graphics, and short-form assets benefits directly from AI video acceleration |
| **television_production** | Television production companies, factual/entertainment producers, and episodic production groups | Repeated episode, promo, highlight, and supporting content workflows are strong fits for AI-assisted video generation |
| **media_production_org** | Major media production organizations with large-scale studio, news, or multi-channel output | Broad production volume and multi-format content demand create strong speed, scale, and cost use cases |
| **short_drama** | Short-form drama producers, vertical video studios, social drama platforms | High-volume short video output is exactly Seedance's sweet spot |
| **ai_tools** | Companies building AI-powered products that incorporate video generation | API integration; embed Seedance into their own product pipeline |
| **creative_agency** | Video-focused creative and advertising agencies | Faster iteration, more variants per brief, lower production cost per client |
| **creative_performance** | Performance marketing teams, creative-performance shops, adtech platforms, and martech companies with video/ad-creative workflows | Seedance can power faster video ad iteration, localization, and variant generation for paid growth and creative testing |
| **content_creator** | High-volume content creators, influencer studios, creator economy businesses | Scale content output without hiring; maintain posting velocity with AI |
| **advertising** | Performance marketing agencies, in-house ad teams with video at scale | AI-generate ad creatives, A/B variants, localised versions at scale |
| **marketing_scale** | Marketing teams at growth-stage or enterprise companies with high video volume | Adjacent — qualify if video production is a stated bottleneck |

## Target Personas (Priority Order)

### Tier 1 — Decision Makers (highest buyer_fit score)
| Persona | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| **Founder / Co-founder** | Full budget authority; often wearing creative + production hat |
| **CEO / Managing Director** | Strategic buyer; often the one feeling cost/speed pain directly |
| **Head of Production / Head of Content** | Owns the workflow that Seedance replaces |
| **VP / Director of Creative** | Budget holder for creative tooling |
| **Executive Producer / Head of Programming Production** | Strong owner of production budgets, schedules, and output quality across shows or channels |
| **CTO / CPO** | If company is building AI products with video component |
| **Head of Product / Product Lead** | Strong buyer when the product owns creative generation, ad tooling, or AI workflow automation |

### Tier 2 — Influencers (medium buyer_fit score)
| Persona | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| **Creative Director** | Strong champion; often drives tool adoption even without direct budget |
| **Senior Producer / Lead Editor** | Feels the day-to-day pain directly; strong internal advocate |
| **Television Director / Broadcast Director** | Strong production-side champion; often has meaningful workflow influence even when procurement sits elsewhere |
| **Product Manager / Group Product Manager** | Strong champion in adtech/martech or AI-creative platforms when they own experimentation, creative tooling, or workflow automation |
| **AI / ML Lead** | Technical evaluator for AI video integration |
| **Senior Designer / Senior Motion Designer** | Knows the tools market; can influence purchasing |

### Tier 3 — Low Priority (low buyer_fit score)
| Persona | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Individual designer / editor / animator (no seniority) | Can flag but not buy |
| Marketing coordinator / content coordinator | Too junior |
| Intern / assistant / junior anything | Do not qualify |

**NOT A TARGET (score buyer_fit 0–5):** Creative Partner, Creative AI Partner — these are partnership/vendor-facing roles with no production budget authority. Do NOT treat them as senior buyers regardless of seniority signals.

**JUNIOR ROLE = HARD DISQUALIFY:** intern, junior, coordinator, assistant, analyst without seniority. Antti's rule: "junior contacts with limited buying power" are NOT targets.

## Core Use Cases

1. **AI video generation at scale** — produce 10× the video output without 10× the headcount
2. **Short-form content production** — daily social/drama clips with AI-generated sequences
3. **Film/commercial post-production automation** — B-roll, transitions, visual effects
4. **Broadcast promo and programming asset generation** — promos, trailers, teasers, cutdowns, explainer segments, and supporting graphics across channels
5. **Ad creative generation** — multiple variants of a video ad from one brief
6. **Creative-performance optimization** — rapid testing of video ad variants, localized cuts, and performance creative
7. **API integration** — embed Seedance into an AI product's video pipeline

## Anti-ICP — Do NOT Qualify

| Type | Reason |
|------|--------|
| Pure B2C individual with no team | No commercial volume |
| Government / public sector | Procurement barriers |
| Finance, legal, HR, insurance | No video production workflow |
| Clinical healthcare | Regulatory barriers |
| Pure SaaS with no content angle | No use case |
| Freelancer (solo, no team) | No commercial scale |
| Academic / non-profit (unless AI research) | No buying motion |
