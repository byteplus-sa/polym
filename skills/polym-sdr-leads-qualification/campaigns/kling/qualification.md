## Scoring Model — Total: 100 points

### Segment Fit (20 points) — PRIMARY FILTER

Kling's primary value is cinematic realism and character fidelity. Prioritize buyers who need quality output, not just speed.

- 18–20: Premium film studio, narrative production company, VFX studio, post-production house, fashion/luxury/beauty brand with video content needs, commercial production company, AI tools company building realism-first video pipeline
- 12–17: Gaming/entertainment studio needing cinematic assets, advertising agency on premium or luxury accounts, photography studio extending stills into video, high-production-value content creator
- 5–11:  General marketing team experimenting with AI video, mid-market brand agency, digital agency with occasional premium work
- 0–4:   No fit — high-volume low-quality content shops, B2C, finance, legal, government, healthcare, SaaS without creative production

### Buyer Fit (20 points) — CRITICAL

Award higher scores for newly appointed creative or production leaders (< 12 months in role).

- 18–20: C-suite, Founder, Co-founder, VP, Head of Production/Content/Creative, VFX, Head of AI, Director of AI, Director of Production/Content/Creative, Creative Technologist, Director of Creative Production, Executive Producer, VFX Supervisor, Director of Photography, AI Stragtegist, Head of Brand, Head of AI Automation, Head of Programming Production, Head of Product / Product Lead for creative-generation or ad-creative workflows OR any senior role newly appointed
- 12–17: Director, Television Director, Broadcast Director, Creative Director, Innovation Director, Director of Innovation, Senior Producer, AI Architect/Architects, AI Engineer/Consultant, AI, Lead Animator, Senior VFX Artist, Art Director, Brand Manager, Senior Manager, Lead, Product Manager / Group Product Manager in adtech, martech, creative-performance, or AI-creative tooling with meaningful workflow influence
- 5–11:  Individual contributor in right department (designer, animator, editor, photographer) with no seniority
- 0–4:   Junior, intern, coordinator, assistant, analyst — no buying power. Partnership/vendor-facing roles with no production budget authority.

### Pain Fit (25 points)

Kling buyers feel pain around visual quality, character consistency, and production cost for premium content.

- 22–25: Explicit pain — needs realistic character animation, can't afford live shoots for all scenes, visual quality of other AI tools insufficient, client demanding photorealistic output
- 14–21: Implied pain — high-end brand campaign coming up, production costs for quality content mentioned, exploring alternatives to traditional VFX pipeline, scaling premium creative production
- 5–13:  Generic creative growth signal with loose connection to video quality or production efficiency
- 0–4:   No pain identified

### Workflow Fit (20 points)

- 17–20: Person POSTED about cinematic AI video, character animation, image-to-video, or realistic motion OR active premium video/film workflow confirmed
- 10–16: Person COMMENTED on AI video content OR adjacent premium creative workflow confirmed OR person LIKED Kling-specific content (realism demos, character work) and their company/role maps strongly to cinematic production
- 4–9:   Person LIKED AI video content OR general creative/production workflow inferred
- 0–3:   No relevant workflow, no signal engagement with video/AI content

### Commercial Fit (15 points)

- 13–15: Established production company or brand (20–5,000 employees), funded studio, recognised agency, or luxury/fashion brand with content budget
- 8–12:  Small independent studio or boutique agency with premium clients, early-stage funded startup with clear production needs
- 3–7:   Very small or unclear scale
- 0–2:   Freelancer, solo operator, nano-company
  Add 1–2 pts if company momentum signal present: hiring for creative/AI roles, new creative leadership, recent brand campaign, expansion into new markets.
